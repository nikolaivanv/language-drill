# DE curriculum — Hammer book-coverage audit vs the PLANNED A1–B2 curriculum (2026-07-15)

First automated run of the book-coverage proposer (PR #580) — the DE pilot from
`docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-15-book-coverage-ledger-design.md`. Unlike the
2026-07-12 prose audit, this pass produced one explicit decision per Hammer
section, judged against the **planned** 98-point curriculum in
`docs/superpowers/plans/2026-07-12-de-a1-b2-curriculum.md` (de.ts is still
disabled). GAPs below are sections the model judged core A1–B2 production
grammar with **no owning planned point** — each needs human triage into a new
point, a rescope, or an exclusion-with-reason before the ledger is committed.

### 4 The articles

- **§`4-2-1`** (Abstract nouns): Detailed rules for definite article vs. zero article with abstract nouns (general concept vs. partial/indefinite sense) — core A2–B1 production grammar not fully covered by de-a1-zero-article (which only covers professions/nationalities/materials/languages) or any other curriculum point.
- **§`4-2-2`** (Other nouns typically used with a definite article): Definite article with substances, meals, diseases, languages, historical periods, arts/sciences, institutions, and nominalized infinitives — nuanced article-use rules beyond what de-a1-zero-article or de-a1-articles-nominative cover.
- **§`4-3`** (The use of articles in generalizations): Article use in generalizations: definite article + singular (Der Mensch …) vs. plural (Die Tannen …) vs. zero article — a distinct construction-level rule not taught by any curriculum point.
- **§`4-4-1`** (Geographical and astronomical names): Article use with geographical and astronomical names (masculine/feminine/plural countries always with article; neuter countries without; mountains, lakes, rivers, streets always with article) — core production grammar with no owning curriculum point.
- **§`4-4-2`** (Use of the article with personal names): Article use with personal names (standard: no article; colloquial/dislocation/case-clarification/individualization contexts: with article) — not covered by any curriculum point.
- **§`4-4-3`** (Geographical and proper names qualified by an adjective): Definite article required when a geographical or proper name is qualified by an adjective (das heutige Deutschland, der junge Heinrich) — not covered by any curriculum point.
- **§`4-6-2`** (Other contexts where a possessive is not used in German): Definite article instead of possessive with abstract nouns denoting human attributes/emotions, and with 'eigen' constructions — not covered by de-b1-dative-reflexive-body (which is scoped to body parts/clothing) or any other curriculum point.
- **§`4-7`** (Other uses of the definite article): Other uses of the definite article: to mark genitive/dative case on otherwise bare nouns (Sie bedarf der Ruhe), distributive sense (3 Euro das Pfund, zweimal die Woche), and always with 'meist' — not covered by any curriculum point.
- **§`4-8-1`** (Nouns used in pairs or enumerations): Zero article with nouns in pairs or enumerations (Tag und Nacht, Form und Inhalt) even where a single noun would require an article — not covered by any curriculum point.
- **§`4-8-4`** (Other contexts where no article is used): Zero article in 'als' phrases, appositional phrases, formulaic official expressions, headlines/ads, and adverbial genitives — only partially overlaps de-a1-zero-article; the als-construction and apposition rules are not taught by any curriculum point.
- **§`4-10`** (Article use with prepositions): Article omission in prepositional phrases qualified by a genitive or PP, omission of indefinite article in formal adjectival prepositional phrases, and article-less use of specific prepositions (ohne, mit part-whole, ab, gemäß, laut, mangels, per, pro, etc.) — not covered by any curriculum point.

## Tooling notes (follow-ups for the propose:book-coverage CLI)

Two robustness findings from this first full-book run, both worth fixing in
`packages/ai` before the ES retrofit:

1. **Duplicate anchors in front-matter files.** Hammer's index repeats the
   chapter anchor as a section anchor in two front-matter files, which makes
   the model (correctly) decide the anchor twice and trips the parser's
   duplicate guard. The audit runner deduped the section list before
   prompting; `propose-book-coverage.ts` builds the same list and needs the
   same dedupe.
2. **"Exactly one of points/excluded/gap" retry loop.** Chapter 11 twice
   emitted all three decision kinds on one section (`11-1-1`) and only passed
   on a third attempt. Either enforce mutual exclusivity in the tool schema or
   resolve by precedence (gap > points > excluded) in the parser instead of
   throwing.
